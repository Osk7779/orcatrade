Impact programs

Three programs. One ocean.

Focused initiatives where foundation contributions go to work.

Program 01

Orca Habitat Protection

Partnering with marine biologists and conservation NGOs to protect critical orca habitats along key trade shipping corridors. Funding acoustic monitoring, vessel speed reduction programmes, and habitat mapping in partnership with regional authorities.

Program 02

Marine Clean-Up Network

Supporting community-led shoreline and ocean clean-up operations across Asia-Pacific and European coastal zones. Each program is tracked with before/after impact data and co-funded by client contributions tied to their sourcing volumes.

Program 03

Ocean Education & Advocacy

Educational programs for coastal communities, schools, and industry stakeholders on the link between global commerce and ocean health. Building the next generation of advocates who understand that trade and conservation are not opposites.

Foundation leadership

The person behind the Foundation.

Sir Timotheus Carrington

OrcaTrade Foundation Lead

Sir Timotheus Carrington brings to the OrcaTrade Foundation something increasingly rare in modern philanthropy: a genuinely long-term perspective. As a descendant of the Aristorcacry lineage — a family with centuries of stewardship across European maritime and natural heritage — he approaches conservation not as a cause to be championed, but as an obligation to be honoured.

His leadership of the Foundation is defined by patience and rigour. Where most conservation initiatives measure success in campaigns, Sir Timotheus measures it in ecosystems — in the slow recovery of orca habitats, the gradual restoration of shipping corridors, the compounding effect of education reaching coastal communities year after year. He has no interest in headline philanthropy. He is interested in outcomes that outlast the people who fund them.

Under his direction, the OrcaTrade Foundation operates with the same discipline applied to any sourcing programme: clearly defined objectives, transparent reporting, and partners held to measurable standards. Conservation funding is allocated across three structured programmes — orca habitat protection, marine clean-up operations, and ocean education — each tied directly to OrcaTrade's commercial activity, so that every shipment facilitated contributes, in a defined and traceable way, to the health of the oceans it crosses.

Sir Timotheus holds advisory relationships with several European marine biology institutions and has co-developed impact frameworks with conservation NGOs operating across the Asia-Pacific and North Atlantic corridors. He is based between London and the Polish coast.
