The €100k–€2M-a-year European importer sits in a market gap so wide that 15–30% of landed cost is silently lost to FX margin, sub-optimal routing, missed preferential-origin pathways, and uncalculated compliance exposure.

OrcaTrade is the operating system for European SMEs importing from Asia. We built this because Polish and German importers in our personal networks were drowning in process — eight different vendors per shipment, five different communication channels, no audit trail when CBAM season hits.

The platform has three layers. Layer one is the AI agent suite — five specialists (sourcing, compliance, logistics, finance) plus an orchestrator that routes across them. Every claim cites either an EU regulation chunk or a deterministic calculator. Twenty-five tools, total.

Layer two is operational tooling: HS-code lookup, customs clearance estimator, bonded-warehouse comparison, six-hub 3PL benchmark, payment-instrument comparison, FX hedging cost calculator. The math is shown to the customer — never a black box.

Layer three is execution through partners. Sample consolidation in our Hong Kong office. Supplier verification on the ground. Forwarder bookings through DSV / Kuehne+Nagel / Röhlig. Insurance via Atradius / Coface. We're asset-light by design.

The thesis is simple: SME importers can't afford a 50-person trade-ops team, but they can pay €99–€999 a month for the AI version of one. We're the layer between Alibaba and the EU shelf.

Section 03 · The Problem

How a typical EU importer operates today

Imagine a Polish e-commerce founder bringing €25,000 of woven apparel from Vietnam to Warsaw. Today, here is how their day actually goes:

StageReality
Sourcing Alibaba browser tabs and WhatsApp threads with three supplier-side sales agents. No single record of who quoted what, when, or against which spec.
Freight Priced ad-hoc through a forwarder's Excel quote that arrives 36 hours after the request. No independent benchmark. No way to compare sea, air, and rail on the same goods.
Customs Clearance happens through a broker the customer has never met. MFN duty appears on the invoice with no way to verify whether a preferential-origin certificate (EUR.1, GSP+, EBA) would have eliminated it.
Compliance A fog. CBAM applies to some of these goods. EUDR will apply from December. REACH SVHC limits are real. None of this is surfaced until the customs broker raises it at the wrong moment.
Working capital Sized by feel. The customer does not know that paying their supplier on T-30 frees up two weeks of cargo value as cash. The FX leg — USD-quoted, EUR-paid — is silently costing 2–4% per shipment.
Net effect: the customer routinely overpays 15–30% of total landed cost relative to what an experienced trade-operations team would deliver, and bears compliance risk they cannot price.

Why incumbents don't solve this

Alibaba is breadth without calibration.

50,000 results for "apparel manufacturer Vietnam". No signal on which is auditable, which has EVFTA-eligible documentation, which has produced for an EU buyer before.

Big forwarders ignore SMEs at our tier.

DSV, K+N, DB Schenker, Maersk, Flexport, Forto, Forto — commercially they cannot staff against a €25k shipment. Sourcing, vetting, and compliance are out of scope.

Enterprise GTM is the wrong shape.

SAP GTS, Oracle GTM, Thomson Reuters ONESOURCE. Six-figure annual licences. Twelve-month implementations. The Polish founder sees these and bounces.

Spreadsheets + email is the actual default.

Works at volume zero. Breaks somewhere between shipment five and shipment fifty, usually after a CBAM-eligible cargo arrives uncalculated.

The gap between "free + chaotic" and "enterprise + €200k/year" is precisely the gap OrcaTrade fills.

Section 05 · Product

What we built

The seven calculator engines

Every output traces to inputs the customer can audit. No black-box AI estimates. Each engine is a pure JavaScript module with full unit-test coverage and is callable as a tool by any agent.

EngineWhat it answers
sourcing-quoteWhich of 5 candidate origins (CN/VN/IN/BD/TR) wins on FOB price × MOQ × lead time × IP risk for this category?
routing-quoteWhat are sea, air, and rail prices and transits for this lane, and which is recommended?
customs-quoteWhat is duty + VAT + brokerage on this HS code into this destination — and is bonded warehousing better?
warehouse-quoteWhich of six EU 3PL hubs (Poznań · Frankfurt · Hamburg · Rotterdam · Barcelona · Prague) is cheapest for this volume profile?
fx-quoteWhat is the FX exposure on a non-EUR-quoted purchase, and is hedging economic at this size?
tco-quoteWhat is annual total-cost-of-ownership including inventory carrying cost at the customer's WACC?
working-capitalWhat is cash tied up in inventory + receivables − payables, and which lever (DPO+30, ship-frequency, etc.) frees the most?

The five AI agents

Each agent has a defined system prompt, a defined toolset, and a defined tier-gate. All run on Anthropic Claude Sonnet 4.6 in production, with the orchestrator surfacing Opus on demand. Every agent ships with a request_human_review tool. Agents do not autonomously commit to bookings or sign contracts.

AgentTierScope
Compliance Agent Free EU regulatory landscape — HS classification, duty + VAT, applicable regimes (CBAM, EUDR, REACH, CE, RoHS, food contact, toy safety, textile labelling). Cites every claim against EUR-Lex.
Sourcing Agent Starter+ Category × origin recommendation, RFQ drafting, MOQ + lead-time clarification.
Logistics Agent Growth+ Sea / rail / air mode comparison, Incoterm advisor, customs clearance route comparison, document checklist.
Finance Agent Growth+ Payment-instrument comparison (LC vs TT vs DA/DP), FX hedge sizing, working-capital optimisation, trade-finance referrals.
Operations Orchestrator Growth+ Meta-agent routing across the four specialists. 14 unique tools after deduplication. The customer dashboard surface.

Tier-quota gating enforces 20 queries/month free, 200 Starter, 1,000 Growth, unlimited at Scale and Enterprise.

Section 06 · The Moat

Four sources of defensibility

In descending order of durability:

01 · Most durable

Calculator-grounded recommendations

Every number in the platform — duty rates, freight cost, FX risk, working-capital impact, landed cost — is computed from a deterministic model the customer can audit. Agents reference outputs by tool call; they do not estimate. When asked "what's the duty on Chinese cold-rolled steel into Poland," the system does not guess — it calls customs-quote, which reads from structured TARIC + AD/CVD data.

Invisible against a flashy demo. Decisive once a customer has been burned by an LLM hallucination.

02

EU-specific compliance knowledge

CBAM, EUDR, REACH, CE, RoHS, AD/CVD, preferential-origin pathways. American AI platforms in this space don't have this depth. European compliance software is enterprise-tier (€200k+/year). The middle is empty.

Replicating this is years of capital-O Operations, not a sprint.

03

Hong Kong physical presence

OrcaTrade Asia Ltd — operated by co-founder Nigel Lam — handles in-person supplier verification, sample consolidation, and dispute resolution. Pure-digital forwarders cannot match this at the SME price point. A chatbot cannot walk into a factory in Shenzhen.

04

AI-native architecture

Five specialised agents plus an orchestrator. Every claim cited against EUR-Lex chunks or a deterministic calculator output. Built day-one with full tool-use plumbing — not a chatbot grafted onto a legacy stack.

The operating principles behind the moat

Calculator-grounded over generativeEvery monetary number traces to a deterministic calculator. If we cannot calculate it, we say so.
Calibrated trust over breadthWe do not index the whole of Alibaba. The future supplier directory ships with 50 vetted suppliers, not 500,000. Editorial restraint is the product.
Citation disciplineRegulatory claims cite EUR-Lex. AD/CVD figures cite the relevant Implementing Regulation by celex number. If we cannot cite it, we don't claim it.
Human-in-the-loop on irreversible actionsAgents do not autonomously sign contracts, commit to bookings, or transmit payments. Customer trust is fragile.
Trilingual by defaultEvery product surface in EN / PL / DE. Polish customers see Polish copy. German customers see German copy. No "international English" fallback.
Asset-light executionNo warehouses, ships, or customs licences. Value lives in the layer above — supplier intelligence, AI tooling, HK presence — not in capital-heavy infrastructure.
OrcaTrade sits in a deliberately empty quadrant: SME-priced, EU-regulation-deep, AI-native, calculator-grounded, with physical Asia presence. The combination is the moat.

Stream 2 · Transactional revenue

Per-shipment execution fees, paid as the customer ships:

  • Service fee per shipment — €150–€2,500 depending on tier and complexity.
  • Freight markup — 8–15% on freight cost, resold from forwarder partners.
  • Customs clearance — per-declaration fee through licensed broker partner (Polish agencja celna).
  • Inspection services — pass-through to QIMA / AsiaInspection with a small coordination fee.
  • Trade-finance origination — % per deal, referred to Tradeshift / Stenn / FreshFi.
  • Cargo insurance — per-shipment commission, panel placement.

Higher subscription tiers receive transactional discounts: 5% off shipment fees on Starter, 10% on Growth, 15% on Scale.

Section 14 · FAQ & Objection Handling

The ten questions everyone asks

Q1 · "Doesn't Alibaba already do this?"

Alibaba is the front door. It's where the SME starts. We're the four steps that follow — verify the supplier, compute the duty, book the freight, finance the deal. Alibaba never surfaces CBAM exposure on a Chinese steel order; we do, on screen one.

Q2 · "Won't Flexport / Forto add this?"

They're forwarder-first by structure. Their commercial teams optimise for shipment volume, not for the SME who imports six containers a year. Compliance and sourcing are out of scope for their ICP. Even if they bolted on a copilot, they wouldn't sit in front of the SME's sourcing decision — they sit downstream of it.

Q3 · "Why won't SAP GTS / Oracle GTM beat you down-market?"

They'll never serve the €99/month customer. Their cost structure assumes six-figure implementations. Down-market is unprofitable for them by design. We're the layer between free-and-chaotic and €200k/year-and-enterprise.

Q4 · "What stops a well-funded US AI startup from copying you?"

Three things they can't quickly replicate: (1) the EU regulatory depth — 14 compliance regimes, 32 trade-defence dossiers, 15 preferential-origin pathways, all EUR-Lex-cited, all calculator-grounded; (2) the Hong Kong physical presence; (3) the trilingual EN/PL/DE surface that an American team can't write in their sleep. The combination — not any one — is the moat.

Q5 · "What's the AI vs. calculator split? Aren't you just an LLM wrapper?"

Every monetary number comes from a deterministic calculator. The LLM never produces a number that drives a business decision. Agents call calculators as tools; they don't estimate. This is the architectural rule, enforced at the import-graph level: no Anthropic SDK imports in /lib/forecasting/, /lib/anomaly/, /lib/inventory/, or /lib/revenue/.

Q6 · "How do you make money beyond subscriptions?"

Transactional. Per-shipment fees (€150–€2,500), 8–15% freight markup from forwarder partners, customs-clearance fees, inspection coordination, cargo-insurance commission, trade-finance referral economics. A Growth-tier customer routinely lands in the €8k–€15k ACV band once they ship at expected frequency.

Q7 · "What's the cost to acquire a customer?"

Pre-traction projection: ~€600 blended CAC against an LTV of €4,500+ on Starter, €15k+ on Growth. SEO content moat is the cheap channel (free per page, expensive in aggregate authorship — but already shipped). Founder outbound is cheap-but-slow. Partner referrals are unpaid and inbound. The economics work as long as content compounds.

Q8 · "What's your biggest risk?"

GTM execution. The technology risk is behind us; the customer-acquisition risk is in front. Our mitigation is (a) a founder team where one founder builds, one sells, one operates Asia; (b) a free tier that captures every Compliance-Agent user into the funnel; (c) a content library that compounds whether or not we spend on ads.

Q9 · "Why are you the team to do this?"

A founder who built the platform end-to-end (calculators, agents, compliance overlay, 1,200-test gate); a COO running EU commercial relationships out of London; a CSCO with physical Hong Kong presence and personal supplier relationships. Three jurisdictions, three languages, and one product. Few teams in the space combine all three.

Q10 · "What happens if Anthropic raises prices, or there's an API outage?"

Calculator engines run independently — agent unavailability degrades to "AI features temporarily unavailable" without breaking pricing, checkout, or saved plans. On pricing: AI cost-of-goods is a single-digit percentage of subscription revenue; meaningful margin pressure requires Anthropic to raise prices 5–10× without competition responding.
