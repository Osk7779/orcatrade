# 05 · Messaging

## Master positioning sentence

**OrcaTrade is the operating system for European SMEs importing from Asia: AI agents and verified supplier infrastructure that take a buyer from "I don't know how to do this" to goods on the warehouse floor.**

This sentence is the source. Every piece of marketing copy traces back to it.

## Brand line

**Find it · Verify it · Ship it · Finance it — one platform, Asia to Europe.**

Used as the homepage hero subtitle and on most channel banners.

## Underlying promise

**Your import operations team, available 24/7 — that knows every supplier, every shipment, every regulation.**

Used in voice-of-customer copy, partner pitches, deck cover.

## ICP value props

Three target segments. Each has a distinct value prop, distinct objections to defuse, and a distinct lead artefact.

### ICP 1 — Polish e-commerce founders

**Who:** Allegro sellers, Amazon FBA operators, multi-channel D2C brands. €50k–€500k annual import volume from Asia.

**Pain:** Patchwork process. Supplier on Alibaba, freight broker email, customs broker on WhatsApp, no single view. CBAM, EUDR, REACH compliance is a black box.

**Value prop:** "Stop running your import process out of email threads. One platform: find the supplier, verify them, ship the goods, stay compliant. Built for Polish SMEs by Polish operators with HK boots on the ground."

**Lead artefact:** Compliance Agent + Routing calculator (free).

**Channels:** Polish-language LinkedIn, Allegro seller communities (Facebook groups), Polish e-commerce Slack.

### ICP 2 — German Mittelstand

**Who:** Manufacturing SMEs sourcing components from China/Vietnam. €500k–€2M annual import volume. Often family-owned, conservative.

**Pain:** Compliance risk is existential. CBAM penalties (Article 26), EUDR scope, REACH SVHC drift — they need precision and audit trail. Big forwarders (DSV, K+N, DB Schenker) ignore them at this volume tier.

**Value prop:** "Regulatory precision at SME pricing. AI agents that cite EUR-Lex, deterministic calculators that show their math, audit trails on every recommendation. Hong Kong physical presence for supplier verification — not a chatbot pretending."

**Lead artefact:** Compliance Agent demo + sample analysis report (the PDF).

**Channels:** German LinkedIn, trade-publication advertorials (Logistik Heute, DVZ), curated webinars with HK partners.

### ICP 3 — Specialty retailers and brand owners

**Who:** Premium DTC brands, niche product specialists, slow-fashion / heritage brands. 5–50 shipments per year, low frequency / high consideration.

**Pain:** Quality is everything; one bad shipment kills brand trust. Need supplier audits, sample QC, returns handling — not just a freight quote.

**Value prop:** "From sample to shelf with the same partner. HK office consolidates samples, third-party AQL on every shipment, returns/refurb when defects happen. Curated supplier shortlist, not the entire Alibaba directory."

**Lead artefact:** Sourcing Agent + sample-management quote (paid trial).

**Channels:** Founder-led LinkedIn, niche industry publications, partner-network referrals.

## Elevator pitches by length

### 10 seconds

> "One platform for European SMEs importing from Asia: find suppliers, ship goods, stay compliant — with AI agents that cite the regulations and HK boots on the ground."

### 30 seconds

> "OrcaTrade is the operating system for SMEs importing from Asia. Most importers run their process out of email threads — supplier on Alibaba, freight broker on WhatsApp, customs broker by phone, no single view. We replace that with one platform.
>
> Five AI agents handle sourcing, compliance (CBAM/EUDR/REACH/CE), logistics (sea/rail/air), customs, and finance. Every recommendation cites a regulation or a deterministic calculator — never guesswork. Hong Kong office for supplier verification on the ground."

### 2 minutes

> "OrcaTrade is the operating system for European SMEs importing from Asia. We built this because Polish and German importers in our personal networks were drowning in process: 8 different vendors per shipment, 5 different communication channels, no audit trail when CBAM season hits.
>
> The platform has three layers. **Layer one** is the AI agent suite — five specialists (sourcing, compliance, logistics, finance) plus an orchestrator that routes across them. Every claim cites either an EU regulation chunk or a deterministic calculator. Twenty-five tools total.
>
> **Layer two** is operational tooling: HS-code lookup, customs clearance estimator, bonded-warehouse comparison, six-hub 3PL benchmark, payment-instrument comparison, FX hedging cost calculator. The math is shown to the customer — never a black box.
>
> **Layer three** is execution through partners. Sample consolidation in our Hong Kong office. Supplier verification on the ground. Forwarder bookings through DSV / Kuehne+Nagel / Röhlig. Insurance via Atradius / Coface. We're asset-light by design.
>
> The thesis: SME importers can't afford a 50-person trade ops team, but they can pay €99–€999/month for the AI version of one. We're the layer between Alibaba and the EU shelf."

## What NOT to say

- "Disrupt" — we're complementing forwarders, not replacing them.
- "AI-first" — descriptive of how we built it, not a claim about the product.
- "Save 50% on shipping costs!!" — we don't make that claim; it's not consistently true.
- "Trusted by industry leaders" — without named logos, this is hollow.
- "Easy", "simple", "seamless" — voice rules apply: show, don't claim.
