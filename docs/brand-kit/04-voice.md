# 04 · Voice

## The one-sentence rule

**OrcaTrade sounds like a senior trade operator who's run Asia-Europe shipments for a decade. Speaks plainly. Cites numbers. Names specific factories, specific HS codes, specific routes. Never generic. Never breathless.**

If a sentence could appear unmodified on Flexport, Forto, or DSV's website, it's not OrcaTrade voice. We earn the read by being more specific.

## Voice axes

OrcaTrade voice sits on these positions:

| Axis | OrcaTrade is | NOT this |
|------|-------------|----------|
| Register | **Operator-direct** | Consultant-vague, advisor-stiff |
| Posture | **Confident** | Apologetic, hedging, "we hope" |
| Specificity | **Numeric, named** | Adjective-stuffed, "unparalleled" |
| Density | **Tight, deliberate** | Listicle-bloated, "5 things you need to know" |
| Emotion | **Quiet authority** | Breathless excitement, "🚀 game-changing" |
| Length | **As long as it needs, no longer** | Padded for SEO, padded for "thought leadership" |

## Voice by channel

| Channel | Register | Sentence length | Notes |
|---------|----------|-----------------|-------|
| Site marketing copy | Operator-direct | 12–24 words | Lead with the verdict, justify with numbers |
| Blog / long-form | Operator-essayistic | Mixed, with occasional one-line hits | Founder's voice, not "content marketing" voice |
| Agent UI hero copy | Operator-precise | 14–20 words | Functional, no fluff |
| Transactional email | Plain, instructive | 6–14 words | "Your CBAM analysis is ready. Open report →" |
| Newsletter | Operator-conversational | Varied, anecdotal | The HK office, the rail terminus, the customs broker — name them |
| LinkedIn | Founder-direct, slightly informal | 6–14 words per line, threaded | Specific stories beat generic insights |
| Ad creative | Sharpened operator | 4–8 words headline, 1 sentence body | Hook with a number or a contrast |

## Words and phrases

### Use freely
- "Most importers don't know that…" (sets up a specific insight)
- "On a typical {weight}-kg shipment from {origin} to {destination}…" (numbers + named places)
- "We benchmark against…" / "We saw {n} of these last quarter"
- "The math is…"
- Specific country and HS-code references

### Use sparingly
- "Game-changing", "revolutionary" — only with proof, never as branding
- "Best-in-class" — never. It's a sentence killer.
- "Trusted by leading…" — only with named logos
- Adjective stacks: "comprehensive · scalable · innovative" — pick one or none

### Don't use
- Em-dashes that aren't actual em-dashes (use `—` not `--`)
- Quotation marks around plain words for emphasis ("efficient" service)
- "Easy", "simple", "seamless" — show, don't claim
- Emoji in marketing copy (technical UIs are fine: ✓ ✗ → ↗︎ ⤴ are OK)
- "We're excited to announce…" — start with the news, not your feelings

## Worked examples

### Hero copy — bad → good

**Bad:** "Revolutionising global trade with AI-powered insights for the modern importer."
- Adjective-stuffed, generic, could be any AI startup, no number.

**Good:** "Most SMEs default to sea or air. Rail is the third option nobody's pitching."
- Specific (sea/air/rail), names a behaviour ("nobody's pitching"), invites curiosity, no adjectives.

### CTA — bad → good

**Bad:** "Get started today!"
- Salesy, vague, exclamation point.

**Good:** "Compare modes →" / "Run the calculator →" / "Open the Orchestrator →"
- Functional, specific, the user knows exactly what happens.

### Email subject — bad → good

**Bad:** "🚀 Big news from OrcaTrade — you won't want to miss this!"
- Emoji, hype, no information density.

**Good:** "CBAM rates updated for Q2: your steel imports are now indicatively €4,200/year"
- Specific number, named regulation, named commodity.

### LinkedIn post — bad → good

**Bad:** "Excited to share that we've launched our new AI agent for compliance! 🎉 #AI #Compliance #Trade"
- Hashtags, exclamation, "excited to share".

**Good:**
> The Compliance Agent went live this week.
>
> 9 tools. 56 chunks of EU regulation text. Cites every claim against EUR-Lex.
>
> Test it on the question we built it to answer:
>
> "1,200 tonnes of rebar from Hangzhou to Gdańsk this year — am I exposed to CBAM?"
>
> orcatrade.com/agent

- Numbers, specifics, opens a loop, no fluff.

## Founder voice (Oskar)

Oskar's voice on LinkedIn is the highest-leverage channel. Adjustments from generic OrcaTrade voice:

- **Slightly more personal** — "I noticed…", "We had a customer last week who…"
- **Operator anecdotes** — short stories from HK office, port visits, regulatory letters
- **No hedging** — Oskar has a strong view; the post has a strong view
- **One-line punchlines** — Cormorant headline energy in the closing line
