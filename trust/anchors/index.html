Audit-chain anchors

Every event OrcaTrade writes — sign-ins, plan saves, org changes, agent escalations — is sealed into a sha256 hash chain at write time. The current chain head is published at /api/audit-anchor; this page renders the rolling history of those heads. Pin any row externally, then come back later — if the chain at that row's sequence number no longer matches the head you saved, the chain was rewritten and we can't hide it.

How to verify externally

The verification protocol is sha256(prevHash + canonical(event)) over a personal-data-free projection of each event. Full algorithm and the projection's exclusion list: docs/security/audit-trail.md.

  1. Fetch /api/audit-anchor now. Save the { chainHead, chainLength, asOf } locally.
  2. Come back any time later. The new chainLength must be ≥ what you saved.
  3. The chain head at your earlier chainLength must still equal what you saved. OrcaTrade ops can re-walk the stored chain via GET /api/audit?format=verify-stored and the result must agree with this page.
  4. If your earlier head no longer matches at its earlier sequence number, the chain was rewritten. The integrity guarantee rides on sha256 — not on trusting our auth surface.
