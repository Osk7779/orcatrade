Cost and lead time at a glance

For consumer electronics sourced from Vietnam, OrcaTrade's benchmark is FOB index 1.05 relative to a Chinese tier-2 factory baseline (CN = 1.00). This means a unit you'd buy at €10 FOB from China typically lands at €10.50 from Vietnam — before duty, freight, and any preferential origin claims.

Production runs 6 weeks at the factory; sea freight from Vietnam to Rotterdam adds 5 weeks. Total door-to-warehouse: 11 weeks. Air freight saves 3-4 weeks at significant cost premium; for the 200-5000 kg sweet spot from China, rail via Małaszewicze is faster than sea by 10-15 days.

MOQ bands: minimum acceptable is around 2000 units, typical orders are 8000 units. Below the minimum, factories often deprioritise small orders and add 1+ week to the lead time.

Specialty and risk profile

Where Vietnam excels

Major brands (Samsung, LG) have driven supplier-network buildout

Watch for

Component supply still partly imported from CN; lead time can ripple

Country context

  • EVFTA gives preferential EU duty access on most categories
  • Rapid production capacity growth since 2018
  • Quality variance higher than CN tier-1 factories

Quality and IP risk

Quality risk: medium. A factory inspection before the first order is recommended, with AQL on the first 3 shipments.

IP risk: medium. Standard NDA + clear ownership clauses on tooling and design files in the master agreement.
